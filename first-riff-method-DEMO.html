Lesson 1 · five minutes in

The First Riff

⏱ 5 minutes · no theory

Before anything else, you're going to sound dangerous.

No tuning lecture, no how-to-hold-a-pick essay. Rest the guitar on your leg and let's make a riff. We're using the two thickest strings — the two closest to the ceiling. Ignore the other four. They don't exist yet.

Step 1 — find the home sound

Pluck the thickest string (the 6th) without pressing anything. That low note is home base. Hit it four times, steady. You're playing.

Step 2 — add one finger

Press the thickest string at the 3rd fret with one finger. Pluck it. A two-note vocabulary — one more than your first riff needs.

Step 3 — play the riff
E|--0---0---3---0----0--3--3---|
   open    3rd      (repeat, slow & ugly)

0 = open, no finger. One string only. Speed is the last thing to arrive, never the first.

🔥

Callus Countdown, Day 1. Your fingertip will sting — that's skin building armor, not damage. About 7–10 short days to toughen. Most quitters quit right here, in the part nobody warned them about. You've been warned: push through one week and the pain never comes back.

The payoff

That low, one-string pattern is the exact engine behind the most famous beginner riffs in rock — the "everyone's first riff" sound. Day one. Five minutes. One finger.

Lesson 2 · the power zone

The Power Chord

⏱ 10 minutes · still no theory

One shape. Move it anywhere. Instant rock.

A power chord is two fingers that make any spot on the neck sound huge — the single most reused shape in rock, punk, and metal. It dodges the hard chords because it doesn't care what key you're in.

Power Chord ("5" shape)

Index finger on the 6th string, 3rd fret.
Ring finger on the 5th string, 5th fret.

Strum just those two strings. Thick and a little mean. That's correct.

Now make it rhythmic — the palm mute

Rest the side of your strumming hand lightly on the strings near the bridge. The sound goes from ringing to a tight chug — 80% of why rock guitar sounds like rock guitar.

      chug chug   CHUG      chug chug  CHUG
E|----3----3-----3---------3----3-----3----|
A|----5----5-----5---------5----5-----5----|

Same shape throughout. The music comes from the rhythm and the mute, not from moving your fingers.

The payoff

Slide that two-finger shape to different frets and you're playing riffs. No chord name, no note, no theory — and you already sound like a band warming up.
